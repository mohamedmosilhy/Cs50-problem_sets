The kamanja Instrument

The violin (called kamanja or kaman in Arabic) was incorporated into the Arabic ensemble toward the end of the 19th century
and replaced indigenous varieties of fiddles like the kamanja, rababa, and jowza. While the argument can be made that the
violin is the most obvious example of an Arabized instrument, it has long been naturalized, and has acquired its own tuning,
playing technique and ornamentation style. The Arabic violin's sound is as esential to Arabic music as that of the oud and
the qanun.
